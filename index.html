Phase 1
03

Days 1-30: Listen, Learn, Map

Understand the full landscape before making any moves.

Relationships

Partner Relationship Audit

  • Schedule introductory or reintroduction calls with every coalition partner (Center for Black Educator Development, Indigitize, Latinos for Education, CSTA, NEA, PlayLab AI) and with the program's funder, Google
  • For each: understand their priorities, what they value most about the partnership, where they see gaps, and what success looks like from their side
  • Document the current state of each relationship: key contacts, what's been promised, delivered, and outstanding
Operations

Program Deliverables Review

  • Map all grant deliverables against the remaining timeline (through late 2027)
  • Identify which deliverables are on track, which are at risk, and which have room for acceleration
  • Meet with the Senior Project Manager to understand current workflows, reporting cadences, and pain points
Program

CoP Cohort Assessment

  • Review participation and engagement data across all active CoP cohorts
  • Identify which cohorts are thriving and which need additional support
  • Talk to facilitators (including those I've worked alongside) about what's working and what's falling flat
Internal

Internal Orientation

  • Understand ISTE+ASCD's internal structure: how Generation AI connects to Custom Learning Services, ISTE U (D2L), the editorial team, and the events team
  • Map the communications infrastructure: newsletter workflow, website CMS, social media coordination
Phase 2
04

Days 31-60: Identify Gaps and Opportunities

Turn the audit into a clear picture of where the program can improve.

Professional Learning

Professional Learning Evolution

  • Assess how well current PL content aligns with ISTE's Transformational Learning Principles and the Profile of an AI Ready Graduate framework (Learner, Researcher, Synthesizer, Problem Solver, Connector, Storyteller). These frameworks should anchor the program's direction as AI evolves.
  • Ensure the professional learning answers "why" before "what." Educators need to understand what agentic AI, multimodal models, and AI-generated content mean for how they design learning, in ways specific to their content area and context.
  • Identify where educators are in their AI readiness. Many schools are still at AI 101. Others are ready for deeper integration. The program should meet both.
  • Build a tighter feedback loop from CoP participants back into resource development. The best use cases are coming from the educators themselves.
Key principle: A math teacher and an ELA teacher should walk away with different applications of the same principle. Context-specific integration matters more than generic AI training.
Partnerships

Partnership Deepening Strategy

  • Based on the audit: identify which partnerships are actively deepening vs. plateauing
  • For each partner, propose one concrete next step that adds value to both sides
  • Assess whether natural partner combinations could create joint programming (e.g., NEA's national reach paired with PlayLab AI's tooling, or Latinos for Education and Center for Black Educator Development co-anchoring an equity-focused cohort)
Pipeline

Facilitator Pipeline Assessment

  • Evaluate the AI Cadre train-the-trainer model as the entry point for a facilitator pipeline
  • Identify high-performing CoP members who could eventually co-facilitate sessions or join CLS faculty
  • Map what a structured pathway from participant to facilitator would look like: selection criteria, training requirements, co-facilitation shadowing, independent facilitation
Communications

Communications Gap Analysis

  • Evaluate the monthly newsletter: open rates, content mix, audience segmentation
  • Review website content for accuracy and engagement
  • Assess conference presence strategy for remaining program timeline
Phase 3
05

Days 61-90: Execute First Initiative

Deliver one concrete improvement that demonstrates momentum. The right choice depends on what Days 1-60 surface.

Option A
If the constraint is facilitator capacity

Facilitator Pipeline Launch

Formalize the AI Cadre as the first cohort of a structured train-the-trainer program. Define selection criteria, develop a facilitator toolkit, and pilot a co-facilitation model where Cadre graduates shadow experienced facilitators in the next CoP convening.

Equity lens: Leverage HBCU networks, including the Morehouse Center for Excellence in Education (Dr. Nina Gilbert) and organizations like the Center for Black Educator Development, to recruit Black educators into the pipeline. The program grows stronger when the people delivering it reflect the communities it serves.
Option B
If the constraint is visibility of impact

Partner Impact Report

Produce a mid-program impact report for coalition partners: educator reach, CoP engagement data, participant outcomes, case studies. This gives partners material they can use internally to justify continued and expanded investment.

Option C
If the gap is content alignment

Content Alignment Sprint

Run a focused sprint to evolve the highest-traffic PL modules. Ground the updates in ISTE's Transformational Learning Principles and the Profile of an AI Ready Graduate framework. Incorporate participant-generated use cases from CoP sessions.

The goal: Professional learning that helps educators think about AI's implications for student learning in ways specific to their context and content area.
